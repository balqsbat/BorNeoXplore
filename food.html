  • 1 cup sago starch (tapioca starch can be a substitute if you can‚Äôt find sago starch)
  • 3 cups hot water
  • Cacah sauce

How to Cook:

  1. Boil water: Bring 3 cups of water to a boil in a pot.
  2. Add starch: Slowly pour in the sago starch while stirring continuously with a wooden spoon or chopsticks to avoid lumps.
  3. Cook: Keep stirring the mixture until it thickens and becomes translucent. It should form a sticky, stretchy, glue-like texture.
  4. Serve: Transfer the ambuyat into a bowl. It‚Äôs best eaten hot.

  • 250g unsalted butter (softened)

  • 200g all-purpose flour

  9. Repeat layering: Continue layering and baking until all batter is used.
